FAQ

I'm not fast enough for a coach. Is Daptiv really for me?
Absolutely. Whether you're chasing a PR, coming back from injury, or just trying to stay consistent, Daptiv meets you where you are and adapts when life doesn't go to plan. Whether you're running 15-minute miles or 7-minute miles, the AI adapts to your body, not some arbitrary standard. You don't need permission to deserve a two-way coaching experience.
I'm concerned about privacy with AI coaching apps. Should I be?
Not with Daptiv. We get it—trusting an AI with your training data is a big ask. Here's our approach: We never sell your data. Period. Your information is anonymized and used only to power your training plan and coaching. We couldn't tie it back to you personally even if we wanted to. You're in control. Delete your data anytime from the app. And we only collect what's needed to coach you effectively. Think of it like a human coach: what you share exists only to help you improve, and your privacy is protected.
Can I actually talk to the AI like a real coach, or is it just canned responses?
Real, contextual conversation. You can explain doubts, ask "why am I doing this workout?", share that your knee felt weird, or admit you're dreading tomorrow's run. The AI maintains context about your training history, goals, and life circumstances so it doesn't just spit out generic advice. It feels more like texting a coach who knows your story.
I'm worried I'll start strong and quit in two weeks like every other time. What's different?
The app is designed to prevent the burnout-quit cycle. Static plans fail because they don't adapt when you miss a workout, get sick, or life gets chaotic—so you fall behind, feel guilty, and quit. Daptiv removes that spiral: miss a run, tell your coach, plan adjusts, you keep going. No guilt. No "falling off the wagon." Just progress at your pace.
How do I know if the training advice is legit, or just AI making stuff up?
Daptiv's training plans are grounded in proven running science and coaching principles, including progressive overload, periodization, recovery cycles. The AI applies these principles to your specific context, so you get evidence-based training that actually fits your life.
What if I miss workouts? Will the plan just pile up and make me feel worse?
No guilt, just recalibration. Unlike static plans that resume as if nothing happened, Daptiv rebuilds your week when life gets happens—sick kids, work deadlines, or just a rough day. You tell your AI coach what happened, and the plan adjusts so you never feel "behind." The goal is progress, not perfection.
I'm coming back from injury/time off. Will this push me too hard too fast?
Daptiv is built for comeback runners. Your onboarding conversation covers past injuries, how long you've been off, and your current fitness. The AI builds at a rate you prefer, listening to your RPE (rate of perceived exertion) and subjective feeling after each run. If something feels off, you say so, and the plan adjusts before you get hurt again.
How much time do I actually need to commit? I have a busy schedule.
You build your schedule, not the other way around. During onboarding, you tell Daptiv which days you can run, how much time you have, and when your long run fits best. The AI works around your life, not against it. And when your schedule implodes (because it will), you can recalibrate mid-week without starting over.
Does this work if I can only run 2-3 times per week?
Yes. Daptiv builds plans for your actual availability, whether that's 3 runs or 6 runs per week. The AI optimizes what you can do, rather than prescribing what you "should" do and setting you up to fail. Consistency on a 3-day plan beats burnout on a 5-day plan every time.
What if I need to add cross training, or run on a treadmill sometimes?
You control the modalities and training context. Mention to your coach that you prefer to bike two days a week during onboarding. And you can chat with your AI coach anytime to adjust for treadmill runs, outdoor conditions, or traveling. The plan adapts to your context, not a one-size-fits-all template.
I've never used an AI coach before. Will it feel weird or robotic?
Most users are surprised how natural it feels. Research shows conversational AI coaching achieves similar trust and engagement as human coaches when it's done right. Daptiv's AI doesn't just answer questions; it remembers your goals, is always available, and adjusts your plan based on actual feedback. It's less "chatbot" and more "coach who doesn't sleep".
Can I ask dumb questions without judgment?
There are no dumb questions. Whether you're wondering why your shins hurt, if you should run in the rain, or what "tempo pace" actually means, the AI coach explains it in context without making you feel like a beginner. You're learning as you go, and that's exactly how it should work.
